## Content:
A takeaway from giving my in-class presentation is to practice speaking before the presentation and to try to speak at a normal pace rather than speaking too fast. So, for me, I feel like I end up speaking too fast, so my presentation ended pretty quickly. This was a learning lesson for me to try and practice speaking a little slower rather than rushing. Another takeaway from giving my in-class presentation is to make sure you're knowledgeable about what you created and what you wrote down on the slide. This is so you won't look at the smart board or the computer too often, but rather make eye contact with the people. Through making eye contact, it shows that you know what you're talking about and it shows that you are engaging with the audience rather than staring at the laptop the whole presentation and reading off a script. This also goes for the Expo elevator pitch, as making sure you're knowledgeable about what you made and wrote is key. The judges wouldn't like you reading off your laptop the whole presentation. This would make them bored rather than engaged. Also, for the Expo elevator pitch, you have to make sure you keep it under the 1-minute to 1-minute and 15-second range. You don't want to speak for too long, as they have to judge other people's presentations.
