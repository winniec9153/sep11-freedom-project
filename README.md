# SEP11 Freedom Project
by **Winnie Chen**

## Context
I am a student at [HSTAT](https://www.hstat.org/) in the [Software Engineering Program](https://hstatsep.github.io/). The "Freedom Project" for SEP11 is a year-long project all about making something using JavaScript along with a third-party JS tool.

For my project, I chose to independently study p5Play in order to help me make my Budgeting App. p5Play is a JavaScript library that adds sprites, animations, and game mechanics to p5.js, making it easier to create 2D games. I used p5Play for the background animation of my app, which made it less boring. I chose the topic of budgeting because nowadays teenagers and adults don't keep track of their finances and spend their money recklessly. Through this budgeting app, they can become more financially organized and manage their money more wisely.

## Implications
##### PROS
* Global (Pros)
  * Big Systems
    * Helps promote financial responsibility by encouraging users to track their spending and income.
    * Can help users develop better budgeting habits and make more informed financial decisions.
* Social
  * Individual
    * Allows users to monitor their spending and earnings over time through a daily transaction log.
    * Makes it easier to understand personal spending habits and stay organized financially.
  * Relationships
    * Can reduce financial stress between family members or partners by helping users keep track of their money.
    * Encourages accountability when managing shared financial responsibilities.
* Economic
  * Jobs
    * This helps the users build money-management skills that are useful in future careers and workplaces.
    * Encourages more responsible financial planning, which can contribute to long-term financial stability.
* Infrastructure
  * it digitizes budgeting which reduces the need for paper records, notebooks, or printed spreadsheets.
  * it provides a simple system for organizing financial information more efficiently.
##### CONS
* Big Systems
  * The daily log is not permanently stored, meaning the financial records are lost whenever the app is restarted.
  * There is a limited functionality which may prevent users from relying on it for long-term financial tracking.
* Social
  * Individual
    * Users cannot review past transactions after closing the app because the data is not saved.
    * The app does not handle negative numbers correctly, which can lead to inaccurate balance calculations.
    * Incorrect calculations may confuse users and reduce trust in the budgeting information.
  * Relationships
    * Inaccurate financial records could cause misunderstandings if users are tracking shared expenses.
    * Lost transaction history may make it difficult to discuss spending habits with family members or partners.
* Economic
  * Jobs
    * There is limited features compared to professional budgeting software which may reduce its usefulness in workplace or business settings.
    * Errors involving negative values can make financial reporting less reliable.
* Infrastructure
  * Since data is not permanently stored, users must repeatedly enter information, making the system less efficient.
  * If there is a lack of data persistence it will reduces the app's effectiveness as a long-term financial management tool.
